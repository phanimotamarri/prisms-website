Integrated Leading Edge Science

The PRISMS Center is organized around four Use Case topic areas: precipitate evolution, recrystallization and grain growth, tensile behavior, and fatigue behavior. These Use Cases serve as demonstrations and test beds for development of the PRISMS framework. They also serve as the focal point for our highly integrated and collaborative scientific workflows and provide a basis and approach for establishing group consensus on goals, priorities and scientific approaches. Faculty and Staff membership in the individual teams are indicated in the narrative portion of this document. These groups meet monthly to plan and review scientific progress and identify gaps and opportunities. Each Use Group has a faculty or staff facilitator who conducts these meetings and assists the group in establishing collaboration plans and reviewing progress. In 2014 each use case group conducted major reviews of goals and collaboration plans as well as the scientific workflow integration protocols for the use group.



Precipitate Evolution

Optimal strength in Mg alloys is achieved by a very particular precipitation sequence exhibited by a variety of Mg-rare earth (RE) alloys. It involves the formation first of Guinier-Preston zones followed by the precipitation of nano-scale hcp-based phases (β’). Further ageing leads to the formation of bcc-based precipitates, referred to as β1, that form from and coexist with the original β’ precipitates. Fundamental questions remain that need to be answered in order to enable the discovery and design of new Mg alloys with optimal strength. For example, i) how can precipitation mechanisms be described theoretically such that the evolution during aging heat treatments can be predicted; and ii) what, in particular, about the rare-earth elements causes this favorable precipitation morphology and orientation and are there other non-rare-earth elements that will exhibit the same behavior. Developing predictive capabilities that address these and other questions is the goal of the PRISMS Precipitate Evolution Use Group. Our initial demonstration of this integrated framework is on β’ and β1 precipitate evolution in a Mg-Nd alloy. This is being conducted in conjunction with the Tensile Behavior use group that will be developing an integrated capability for predicting the influence of precipitates on strengthening and ductility of Mg-RE alloys.



Recrystallization and Grain Growth

The influence of grain size, grain size distribution and grain orientations have a well-documented influence on the mechanical properties of metal alloys. Thus developing and demonstrating a capability for predicting these characteristics is an important PRISMS capability. Due to resource constraints the primary activity of this use case group is planned to occur in the next two years. This use case also represents an opportunity to demonstrate the capabilities of a new computational methodology, Phase Field Crystal.



Tensile Behavior

The Tensile Behavior Use Case group is investigating and developing predictive capabilities for a wide range of complex phenomena including dislocation-precipitate and dislocation-dislocation interactions in this hcp system, prediction of alloying effects on solid solution strengthening and twin formation, prediction and measurements of strain distributions and the propensity for cracking at grain and twin boundaries. An important aspect of the PRISMS Center is the integration of detailed microstructural simulations with property models and it is in this use case that this is being accompliplished.



Fatigue Behavior

The Fatigue Behavior Use Case group is investigating and developing capabilities for predicting the role of alloying and microstructure on the low cycle fatigue response and the growth of microstructurally small fatigue cracks in Mg alloys.
