Fatigue Behavior

The accurate prediction of fatigue damage accumulation in materials subjected to cyclic deformation, known as fatigue, is a critical technological problem and a long-standing scientific challenge. In the Fatigue Behavior Use Case we are developing capabilities for predicting the role of alloying and microstructure on the low cycle fatigue response and the growth of microstructurally small fatigue cracks in Mg alloys. Fatigue is a property that is particularly sensitive to extremes of microstructure and quantifying these microstructural influences in a statistically significant manner is limited by conventional fatigue testing apparatus. To achieve statistically robust fatigue information, UM has pioneered novel diagnostic techniques integrated with unique ultrasonic fatigue (UF) instrumentation that can accelerate fatigue testing by a factor of 500-1000 times (See Experimental Tools). The results of these experiments are being used to parameterize our crystal elasticity and plasticity models to answer questions such as how do fatigue cracks propagate across grain boundaries and how do these boundaries influence the fatigue crack growth rates.

Integration Approach

In the PRISMS integrated scientific workflow integration protocol the outputs from CASM calculations are used as inputs to ParaDis and PRISMS-Crystal Elasticity/Plasticity with Variational Multi-Scale Method used for incorporating traction separation laws for slip band formation and cracking. Microstructure is passed to this use case using Phase Field models for precipitate evolution and grain growth. Key experimental measurements for calibration and validation of these models, include local crack path orientations, fatigue crack growth rates and low cycle fatigue lives.
