Tensile Behavior

The Tensile Behavior Use Case group is investigating and developing predictive capabilities for a wide range of complex phenomena including dislocation-precipitate and dislocation-dislocation interactions in an hcp system, prediction of alloying effects on solid solution strengthening and twin formation, prediction and measurements of strain distributions and the propensity for cracking at grain and twin boundaries. An important aspect of the PRISMS Center is the integration of detailed microstructural simulations with property models and it is in this use case that this is being accompliplished.

Integration Approach

The figure above provides a schematic of the PRISMS Tensile Behavior integration protocol. As indicated in the figure, outputs from statistical mechanics calculations are used as inputs to ParaDis and VMM. In turn, the ParaDis calculations will be used as inputs to the PRISMS-CPFE and PRISMS-Plasticity Codes. Key experimental measurements have also been identified for inputs to CPFE and VMM. The following are some of the key variables that will be captured and passed.

Inputs to Dislocation Dynamics (ParaDis) Simulation
  • Peierls Barrier: To include solute hardening, the Peierls barrier as a function of solute concentration will be calculated using the gamma-surface approach in CASM. Long term it is anticipated that this may be accomplished using RS-DFT.
  • Dislocation core energy: Core energies for edge and screw type dislocations are being calculated using real space RS-DFT.
  • Dislocation glide mobility: Published values determined from molecular dynamics calculations will be used.
  • Precipitate microstructures: For microstructures with precipitates, the microstructure will be obtained from phase field simulations and spatial distribution of the microstructure will be passed to DD.
Inputs to PRISMS-CPFE
  • Hardening coefficients: The crystal plasticity hardening laws are parameterized from large-scale DD calculations that characterize forest hardening and precipitation hardening.
  • Hardening coefficients for twinning in Mg: These coefficients will be determined by fitting models to macroscopic and microscale SEM-DIC experimental results. A computational model for twinning using gradient elasticity is also under development to serve this purpose.
  • Inputs from PRISMS-PF for grain sizes.
Inputs to PRISMS-Plasticity

Yield function, yield stress and hardening laws will be obtained by homogenizing the Crystal Plasticity computations over multiple grain orientations at a continuum point.

Inputs to PRISMS-VMM

Traction separation laws will be estimated using CASM. Issues of mode-mixity, crack initiation criterion and crack propagation criterion are still open challenges, and thus we propose to use any such values/criterion in conjunction with experimental observations.

Computational Tools

  • PRISMS KS-DFT: Real Space Kohn-Sham Density Functional theory code
  • PRISMS PF: Phase field methods code
  • PRISMS Plasticity: Continuum Plasticity and Crystal Plasticity code
  • PRISMS MC: Mechano-Chemical phase transformations code
