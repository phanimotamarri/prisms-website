Tensile Behavior

The Tensile Behavior Use Case group is investigating and developing predictive capabilities for a wide range of complex phenomena including dislocation-precipitate and dislocation-dislocation interactions in an hcp system, prediction of alloying effects on solid solution strengthening and twin formation, prediction and measurements of strain distributions and the propensity for cracking at grain boundaries. An important aspect of the PRISMS Center is the integration of detailed microstructural simulations from other use groups with property models.

Integration Approach

In the PRISMS Tensile Behavior integration protocol outputs from statistical mechanics calculations are used as inputs to the dislocation dynamics code, ParaDis and to traction separation laws using in the Variational Multiscale Method. In turn, the ParaDis calculations will be used as inputs to the PRISMS-Crystal Plasticity and PRISMS-Plasticity Codes. Key experimental measurements come from in-situ SEM-DIC measurements of grain orientations and local cracking, slip and grain rotations.
