Recrystallization and Grain Growth

The influence of grain size, grain size distribution and grain orientations have a well-documented influence on the mechanical properties of metal alloys. Thus developing and demonstrating a capability for predicting these characteristics is an important PRISMS capability. This use case also represents an opportunity to demonstrate the capabilities of a new computational methodology, Phase Field Crystal for predicting grain boundary energies.

Figure: Electron backscatter diffraction image showing crystal orientations of grains in a Mg-Nd-Y alloy (A. Murphy, PRISMS Center).


Integration Approach

The scientific workflow Phase Field Crystal, PRISMS-Crystal Plasticity and PRISMS Phase Field and critical experiments. The primary outputs to other use cases is in the form of PRISMS-PF simulations of critical microstructural parameters such as grain size and grain size distribution.

Figure: Phase Field Crystal calculation of grain boundary energy versus orientation compared with molecular dynamics simulation (J. Luce, PRISMS Center; molecular dynamics simulation courtesy E. Holm, Carnegie-Mellon University).

Using the phase-field crystal method, grain boundary energy will be calculated as a function of the orientations of two grains. This data will be used to parameterize a phase-field model for grain growth that allows for anisotropic grain boundary energy. To determine the mobility of grain boundaries undeformed polycrystalline sample are annealed and the grain size as a function of time measured and from the growth kinetics the mobility can be estimated. Recrystallization rates of deformed microstructures are also determined experimentally for parameterization of phase field models for recrystallization.
