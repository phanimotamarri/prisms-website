Recrystallization and Grain Growth

The influence of grain size, grain size distribution and grain orientations have a well-documented influence on the mechanical properties of metal alloys. Thus developing and demonstrating a capability for predicting these characteristics is an important PRISMS capability. Due to resource constraints the primary activity of this use case group is planned to occur in the next two years. This use case also represents an opportunity to demonstrate the capabilities of a new computational methodology, Phase Field Crystal.

Integration Approach

As indicated in the figure above, the scientific workflow includes CASM, Phase Field Crystal, PRISMS-CPFE, PRISMS-PF and critical experiments. The primary outputs to other use cases is in the form of PRISMS-PF simulations of critical microstructural parameters such as grain size and grain size distribution. Inputs to Phase-Field Model from Simulations: Using the phase-field crystal method, grain boundary energy will be calculated as a function of the orientations of two grains. This data will be used to parameterize a phase-field model for grain growth that allows for anisotropic grain boundary energy. Inputs to Phase-Field Model from Experiment: To determine the mobility of grain boundaries critical experiments are necessary. Undeformed polycrystalline sample are annealed and the grain size as a function of time measured and from the growth kinetics the mobility can be estimated.

Computational Tools
  • PRISMS CASM: Statistical Mechanics code
  • PRISMS KS-DFT: Real Space Kohn-Sham Density Functional theory code
  • PRISMS PF: Phase field methods code
