Recrystallization and Grain Growth

The influence of grain size, grain size distribution and grain orientations have a well-documented influence on the mechanical properties of metal alloys. Thus developing and demonstrating a capability for predicting these characteristics is an important PRISMS capability. This use case also represents an opportunity to develop and demonstrate the capabilities of a new computational methodology, phase-field crystal for predicting grain boundary energies.

Integration Approach

The scientific workflow for prediction of recrystallization and grain growth includes phase-field crystal, PRISMS-Crystal Plasticity and PRISMS Phase Field and critical experiments. The primary outputs to other use cases is in the form of PRISMS-PF simulations of critical microstructural parameters such as grain size and grain size distribution.


Figure: Grain boundary energy calculated from a phase-field crystal model versus misorientation compared with molecular dynamics simulation (J. Luce PRISMS Center; molecular dynamics simulation courtesy E. Holm, Carnegie-Mellon University).


Using the phase-field crystal method, misorientation dependence of grain boundary energy will be calculated as a function of the misorientations of two grains. This data will be used to parameterize a phase-field model for grain growth that allows for misorientation-dependent grain boundary energy. To determine the mobility of grain boundaries undeformed polycrystalline sample are annealed and the grain size as a function of time measured and from the growth kinetics the mobility can be estimated. Recrystallization rates of deformed microstructures are also determined experimentally for parameterization of phase field models for recrystallization.
