Integrated, Focused, Open

The PRISMS Center is built around 3 focus areas. These drive everything we do. All 3 areas are integrated together to make sure each is supporting the others.

  • Integrated Science combines experimental and computational approaches.
  • Computational Tools is an open source framework of integrated, multi-scale tools
  • Materials Commons provides collaboration and storage

Integrated Science

Combining the efforts of computational and experimental researchers, the goal of this Center is development of an integrated suite of validated computational tools that are designed to accelerate the development of new structural metals.

  • Linking Experiments & Simulations
  • Advanced Quantitative Experiments
  • Use Cases
    - Microstructural Evolution
    - Mechanical Behavior

PRISMS Computational Tools

The PRISMS Computational Tools suite is an integrated set of codes for writing large scale models that pass data across the different length scales. The tools are based on standards such as DEAL.II and ParaDIS while adding new models.

  • Open Source
  • Integrated Hierarchical Multi-Scale
  • Computationally Efficient
  • Extensible

The Materials Commons

Materials Commons is a data repository and collaboration platform. It allows researchers to collaborate, tracks the history of your data (provenance tracking) and provides a powerful model for viewing your data.

Overview

Our Center is formally known as the DOE Software Innovation Center for Integrated Multi-Scale Modeling of Structural Metals. We call it PRISMS for short which stands for PRedictive Integrated Structural Materials Science. Our ambitious goal is for our PRISMS tools to be the extensible scientific core of the Integrated Computational Materials Engineering.

About PRISMS

Combining the efforts of computational and experimental researchers, the goal of this Center is development of an integrated suite of validated computational tools that are designed to accelerate the development of new structural metals.

Our Center is formally known as the DOE Software Innovation Center for Integrated Multi-Scale Modeling of Structural Metals. We call it PRISMS for short which stands for PRedictive Integrated Structural Materials Science. Our ambitious goal is for our PRISMS tools to be the extensible scientific core of the Integrated Computational Materials Engineering.
