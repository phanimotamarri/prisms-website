SEM-DIC

The Daly group has developed (under DOE-BES Award No. DE-SC0003996 and NSF Grant No. 092753) a means for full-field, high resolution mapping of deformations at the microstructural length scale using a combination of scanning electron microscopy and digital image correlation (termed here as SEM-DIC). The Daly group created chemically functionalized nanoparticle self-assembly methods for unprecedented spatial resolution, and developed a framework originally proposed by Sutton et al. (Sutton et al., Experimental Mechanics, 2007, 2-part paper series) to correct for the complex spatial and temporal distortions inherent in SEM imaging. These techniques, combined with corrections for stress relaxation and hardware/software developments for various thermo-mechanical testing parameters, enable the acquisition of highly accurate and statistically relevant sub-grain measurements of deformation. Distortion-corrected SEM-DIC provides the accuracy, spatial resolution, and large amounts of data needed to perform quantitative characterization linking microstructure to deformation.
