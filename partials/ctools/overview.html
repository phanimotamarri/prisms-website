PRISMS Multi-scale Framework

The PRISMS Center software development is based on an integrated suite of multiscale/multiphysics computational codes developed by the various research groups affiliated with the center and are facilitated by the research staff and faculty in a highly collaborative environment. The codes are hosted at GitHub and are subject to our evolving best practices and software quality assurance (SQA). Code reviews are regularly conducted and a broadly two stage annual release plan involving (I) internal beta release to PRISMS members and collaborators, and (II) open-source public release under the GNU Lesser General Public License (LGPL) is followed. Stay tuned for our first public release scheduled for Summer 2015.


The PRISMS computational tools are designed to provide a general numerical framework for solutions to the gamut of multiscale/multiphysics problems encountered in the nano-micro-mesa-macro analysis of structural materials. However, given the vast generality and complexity of analytical/numerical/experimental challenges possible across these scales, the Center primarily focusses on three scientific challenges: (I) Predicting the role of alloy composition and processing on microstructure evolution (Hyper link to Precipitate evolution and Recrystallization and Grain Growth use cases), (II) Prediction of tensile properties from first principles (Hyper link to Tensile properties use case), (III) Prediction of fatigue behavior (Hyper link to Fatigue behavior use case). These challenges are addressed by a combination of advanced numerical frameworks (hyper link to codes), experimental techniques (hyper link to tools) and relevant phenomenological models.
