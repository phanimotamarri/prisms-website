PRISMS Integrated Software Framework


The PRISMS integrated computational codes for multiscale materials modeling allow for fast development of large-scale numerical frameworks for various mathematical models of material behavior (Continuum Plasticity, Crystal Plasticity, Phase Field, Statistical Mechanics, DFT, etc). Our tools provide scalable high performance computing (HPC) capabilities for solving large-scale multiphysics problems and model integration across different length scales.


Even better, all our tools are scheduled to be released as open source under the GNU Lesser General Public License (LGPL) license. You will be able to take these leading edge tools, peak under the hood to see how we do things, and integrate them into your research.


At the PRISMS-Center we strongly believe in building an active and collaborative research community for a sustainable development and use of our suite of material modeling tools. If you are interested in collaborating and participating in codes development head on over to our contact page and drop us a line. Building a community and solving hard modeling problems is important to us.


Below you can see a list of the computational codes under development, which will soon be released as open source. Each of these codes can be used stand alone or in an integrated environment. To support the integration, we also provide an integration framework you can use to customize and integrate these tools into your existing computational ecosystem.


PRISMS DFT: Real Space Kohn-Sham Density Functional Theory Code

V. Gavini, S. Rudraraju

Electronic structure calculations have played a significant role in the investigation of materials properties over the past few decades. In particular, the Kohn–Sham approach to density functional theory (DFT) has made quantum-mechanically informed calculations on ground-state materials properties computationally tractable, and has provided many important insights into a wide range of materials properties.

As part of the PRISMS software suite, we are developing a finite element (FEM) based real-space numerical framework for modeling Kohn-Sham DFT. Our primary focus is on accurate electronic structure study of the energetics of defects in crystalline materials using computational domains (with non-periodic/semi-periodic/periodic geometries) containing tens of thousands of atoms. PRISMS-DFT provides us a first principles framework for determining relevant material properties.



CASM: Statistical Mechanics Code

A. Van der Ven, B. Puchala

CASM, a Clusters Approach to Statistical Mechanics, is the PRISMS statistical mechanics software. We have released an initial private version for internal use and beta testing by trusted collaborators. This release provides functionality for symmetry identification, specification of composition axes, enumeration of symmetrically unique supercells and configurations, automatic VASP job submission and results collection, use of automatically determined or custom reference states for formation energy calculation, and convex hull identification.


PRISMS PF: Phase Field Modeling Code

K. Thornton, S. Rudraraju, L. Aagesen

Phase Field Models have been central to computational modeling of complex morphological evolution in multiphase solids. Here at the PRISMS-Center, we are developing a massively scalable Phase Field modeling framework based on the deal.ii open-source finite element library which provides extensive support for various finite element constructs, peta-scale grid generation with adaptivity (p4est adaptive octree meshes), scalable data structures and solvers. While the framework is expected to serve as a generic phase field library with coupled mechanics capability, the initial development focuses on modeling of precipitate evolution in binary and ternary alloys, as well as grain growth and recrystallization.

Current code modules include matrix-free implementations of coupled Cahn-Hilliard, Allen-Cahn and Mechanics, with example demonstrations of spinodal decomposition, chemo-mechanical boundary value problems, and beta-prime precipitate evolution in magnesium/rare-earth binary alloys. The parallel performance and scaling has been demonstrated on terascale problems with billions of degrees of freedom running on thousands of processors.



PRISMS Plasticity: Continuum Platicity and Crystal Plasticity Codes

K. Garikipati, V. Sundararaghavan, S. Rudraraju

The PRISMS-Plasticity code is a parallel numerical framework for modeling continuum scale elasto-plastic material behavior important to the multiscale materials modeling effort of PRISMS. PRISMS-Plasticity encompasses two sub-codes focused on modeling elasto-plastic deformation at the macro-scale (Continuum Plasticity) and meso-scale (Crystal-Plasticity). The two sub-codes are integrated under a common code base for Elliptic Boundary Value problems and is build on top of the deal.ii open-source finite element library.

Currently available models of crystal plasticity include FCC and BCC, and work is under progress for supporting HCP crystals. Under continuum plasticity, we currently support J2-flow theory with isotropic hardening, and include the following isotropic non-linear hyper-elastic models: Quad-Log, Neo-Hookean, St. Venant Kirchoff.



PRISMS-Integration Tools: IntegrationTools and IntegrationToolsWriter

B. Puchala

Integration of PRISMS Codes and external codes is an important aspect of the PRISMS Integrated Framework. To accomplish this the IntegrationTools package was developed and includes the IntegrationTools header file library that defines and implements an API for easily passing arbitrary functions and fields, referred to as ‘PFunctions’ and ‘PFields’, between different software programs. A separate software package, IntegrationToolsWriter was also developed and includes programs that generate and compile the C++ code for PFunctions from symbolic mathematical expressions. We are using this within PRISMS to facilitate passing parameters between codes that operate at different scales in our integrated multi-scale model.

PRISMS MechanoChemo: Mechano-Chemical Phase Transformations Modeling Code

K. Garikipati, A. Van der Ven, S. Rudraraju

We are developing a modeling approach for phase transformations that are driven by free energies that are non-convex in the strain-composition space by using a coupled formulation of finite strain mechanics and diffuse interface theories (Cahn-Hilliard and Allen-Cahn). This work is expected to support the PRISMS material modeling efforts on two fronts: (I) Provide an alternative model for studying precipitate evolution, and (II) Provide a framework for capturing the complex kinematics of twin microstructure formation.


PRISMS PFC: Phase Field Crystal Modeling Code

K. Thornton

The Phase Field Crystal (PFC) model is a simulation approach that can model crystalline materials with atomistic resolution but at a diffusive timescale. We are currently working on developing an in-house research code.
