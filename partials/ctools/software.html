PRISMS Integrated Computational Codes


The PRISMS Integration Computational Codes make developing large scale models easier. Our tools take care of scaling and passing data across the different length scales. Best of all our suite of codes will soon be open sourced. You will be able to take these leading edge tools, peak under the hood to see how we do things, and integrate them into your research.


At the PRISMS-Center we care about the research community. If you are interested in collaborating and participating in codes development head on over to our contact page and drop us a line. Building a community and solving hard modeling problems is important to us.


Below you can see a list of the integrated codes we will be releasing into the community. Each of these can stand alone. They are also designed to be used together. We have an extensive integration framework you can use to customize and integrate these tools into your environment.


PRISMS CASM: Statistical Mechanics Code

A. Van der Ven

CASM, a Clusters Approach to Statistical Mechanics, is the PRISMS statistical mechanics software. We have released an initial private version for internal use and beta testing by trusted collaborators. This release provides functionality for symmetry identification, specification of composition axes, enumeration of symmetrically unique supercells and configurations, automatic VASP job submission and results collection, use of automatically determined or custom reference states for formation energy calculation, and convex hull identification.


PRISMS Plasticity/PRISMS-CPF E: Coninuum and crystal plasticity code

K. Garikipati, V. Sundararaghavan

The PRISMS-Plasticity code is a parallel numerical framework for implementing continuum and crystal plasticity models important to the multiscale materials modeling effort of PRISMS.


PRISMS VMM: Variational Multi-Scale Model

K. Garikipati, V. Sundararaghavan

The ability to carry out computations of crack and shear band evolution is being developed using variational multiscale methods to represent two-dimensional discontinuous displacements.


PRISMS RS-DFT: Real Space Density Functional Theory Code

V. Gavini

To enable an accurate electronic structure study of the energetics of defects in crystalline materials we are developing real-space methods that are capable of handling simulation domains containing tens of thousands of atoms (in order to accurately capture both the electronic-structure of the core and the long-ranged elastic fields).


PRISMS PFC: Phase Field Crystal

K. Thornton

The Phase Field Crystal (PFC) model is a simulation approach that can model crystalline materials with atomistic resolution but at a diffusive timescale


PRISMS MC: Mechano-chemical phase transformations

K. Garikipati, A. Van der Ven

As another advanced code, we have developed a modeling approach for phase transformations that are driven by free energies that are non-convex in composition space by using a coupled formulation of finite strain mechanics and diffuse interface theories (Cahn-Hilliard and Allen-Cahn). This code is being developed using the deal.ii finite element libraries.


PRISMS IntegrationTools: IntegrationTools and IntegrationToolsWriter

B. Puchala

Integration of PRISMS Codes and external codes is an important aspect of the PRISMS Integrated Framework. To accomplish this the IntegrationTools package was developed and includes the IntegrationTools header file library that defines and implements an API for easily passing arbitrary functions and fields, referred to as ‘PFunctions’ and ‘PFields’, between different software programs. A separate software package, IntegrationToolsWriter was also developed and includes programs that generate and compile the C++ code for PFunctions from symbolic mathematical expressions. We are using this within PRISMS to facilitate passing parameters between codes that operate at different scales in our integrated multi-scale model.
